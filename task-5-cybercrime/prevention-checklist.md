# 🛡️ UPI Safety & Prevention Checklist
> **Digital Literacy Project:** Task 5 - Part B  
> **Prepared by:** Student Digital Ambassador

---

### 🚨 5-Point Safety Protocol

1. **The "No-PIN" Rule:** Always remember that a **UPI PIN is only required to SEND money**, never to receive it. If a transaction screen asks for your PIN when someone is supposedly paying you, it is a scam.

2. **Verify Header Details:** Before tapping 'Pay' or 'Proceed', always verify the **Merchant Name** and the **Amount** displayed on the confirmation screen. Scammers often use names like "Verified Buyer" to mask their identity.

3. **Decline Unknown Requests:** If you receive a "Collect Request" notification on your UPI app from an unknown person, **Decline** it immediately. Do not engage with the request or the sender.

4. **Avoid WhatsApp QR Codes:** Never scan a QR code sent over WhatsApp or SMS to "receive" a payment. QR codes are intended for making payments at physical stores or verified gateways, not for receiving funds from strangers.

5. **Secure Your Device:** Enable biometric authentication (Fingerprint/Face ID) for all payment apps. This adds a critical layer of security that prevents unauthorized "Collect Request" approvals if your phone is accessed by others.

---

### 📞 Official Reporting Channels

If you or someone you know has been a victim of a UPI scam or any cybercrime, use these official channels immediately:

* **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Call **1930** (Available 24/7 across India).
* **Official Portal:** File a formal complaint at [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in).
* **Bank Incident Report:** Immediately call your bank's dedicated fraud department to freeze your UPI ID and linked accounts.
* **App-Based Reporting:** Use the "Report Fraud" or "Help" section within your UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) to flag the transaction ID.
